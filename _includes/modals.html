Predicting Molecular Properties

Go to kaggle competition
In this competition, CHAMPS (CHemistry and Mathematics in Phase Space) provided the 3D-structures of 130775 molecules. The goal was to predict the scalar-coupling-constant (a measure of electromagnetic interaction) of atom-pairs. It was extremely interesting to experiment with different ways of representing 3D structures and gathering experience with graph-convolutional networks. Most of the things I tried didn't work very well :-) but in the end, optimizing a standard graph-conv-net for the task at hand got me a place in the top 8% and my first kaggle-medal.

Histopathologic Cancer Detection

Go to kaggle competition

This projcet was done in collaboration with my collegue Marcel as our course project for the seminar 'Advanced Data Challage'.

An interesting aspect of this project was that we trained a model to do a task we couldn't do manually. Trying to detect chancer cells by looking at the images we found that our performance was as poor as random guessing, wheras even our worst models reached ~80% accuracy. This is in stark constrast to most computer vision problems where even the best models don't reach the performance of regular people.

In the end, we used a 121 layer deep DenseNet architecture for this task and managed to obtain a 96% accuracy (binary classification, balanced classes).
